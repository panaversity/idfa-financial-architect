CRITERION: Risk Awareness
The output must surface key assumptions, limitations, or scenarios where the conclusion would change. Presenting results as certain when they depend on fragile assumptions is a FAIL.

OPERATION-SPECIFIC GUIDANCE:
- whatif: PASS = states which assumptions are held constant and what would change if they didn't hold (e.g., "This assumes COGS efficiency stays at 1%/year — if it degrades, GP impact is smaller")
- goalseeking: PASS = notes the sensitivity ("Small changes in COGS% shift the required revenue significantly") and any convergence limitations
- audit: PASS = for violations, explains the business risk of each (e.g., "Coordinate references break when rows are inserted")

PASS DEFINITION:
- Identifies at least one material assumption or limitation
- Explains what would change if that assumption doesn't hold
- Risk is specific to THIS model, not generic boilerplate

FAIL DEFINITION:
- Presents results as certain with no caveats
- Only includes generic disclaimers ("past performance doesn't guarantee future results") not tied to the model
- Ignores obvious sensitivity (e.g., a whatif that changes one variable but doesn't mention others are held constant)
